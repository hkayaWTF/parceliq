§ 01

The Problem

When a real estate agent, developer, architect, or homebuyer wants to understand what they can actually do with a property, they face weeks of manual research, consultant fees, and dead ends. The information exists — scattered across dozens of municipal databases, federal portals, and county records — but nobody has assembled it into one intelligent, actionable package.

🏛️
Zoning Research is Fragmented

Every municipality has its own zoning code. Some are digital. Many are scanned PDFs from the 1980s. An attorney charges thousands just to interpret them for a single parcel.

$2,000–$8,000 per parcel
⏱️
Timelines Are Brutal

A full due-diligence report on a commercial property takes 3–6 weeks between lawyers, environmental consultants, architects, and title companies. Deals fall apart waiting.

3–6 weeks average
🧱
Small Buyers Are Left Out

A family buying their first home has no access to the research a developer would commission. They discover flood zones, historic restrictions, or setback issues after signing.

Zero access to professional intelligence
🗺️
Design Potential Never Visualized

Even professionals rarely commission conceptual design during due diligence. Buyers have no visual sense of what a property could become before committing capital.

Invisible opportunity cost
⚠️
Variances Go Unidentified

Most buyers never learn that a restriction has a variance pathway — a formal legal process to obtain an exception. Attorneys know. Everyone else doesn't. Millions lost.

Unknown variance opportunities
📋
No Single Source of Truth

FEMA, EPA, the county assessor, historic preservation, wetlands — each is a separate portal. Nobody aggregates all of this for a single address. Until now.

§ 03

Agent Architecture

Hub-and-spoke model. One orchestrator receives the address, validates and geocodes it, then dispatches six specialized sub-agents to work in parallel. Each is purpose-built with its own toolset and API access, returning structured JSON for final synthesis.

// Layer Zero — Orchestrator
The Master Controller

Receives raw input, geocodes via Google Maps API, validates the parcel, determines jurisdiction (city, county, state), and dispatches all sub-agents simultaneously via async task queue. Collects all outputs, detects conflicts or gaps, triggers retry logic on failure, and hands the full dataset to the Synthesis Agent for narrative generation.

Synthesis Layer

After all 6 agents report back, a final Synthesis Agent (Claude Opus / GPT-4o) receives all structured JSON, resolves conflicts, writes the full narrative report in plain English, identifies the top 5 action recommendations, and hands the complete package to the PDF generation pipeline.

The Zoning RAG Pipeline

The hardest engineering problem is zoning interpretation. The solution: ingest municipal zoning code PDFs, chunk them, embed into a vector DB, and use RAG to answer questions with source citations. ParcelIQ knows which municipality's code to retrieve based on the geocoded address — making the entire process automatic.
